# Help for `redmer/link-beoordeelaar`

> Language: ENGLISH (this), [NEDERLANDS](nl.md)

You have been given a session link that lets you rate (categorize) a number of pages (URLs).
For each of the pages to be rated, you may give one or more _answers_.
When you're done, press <kbd><samp>Save 💾 and next</samp></kbd> to continue.

Multiple people may work simultaneously on a single session.
You'll see your combined progress on the top-left.

You answers may be incomplete: you can always press <kbd><samp>Save 💾 and next</samp></kbd> and finish judgment at a later time.
That page will simply come back randomly for you or someone else.

Your contact may also have prepared multiple session links that rate one group of pages (URLs).
Then you'd be able to first rate the news pages, whereas the session link for blogpost and news pages will then be able to skip those news pages.

#### Usage

When you start the session, **a popup window opens**.
While rating pages it is convenient to have two windows open:

- the green window with information about the link and the answer options;
- the popup window in which the link to be rated is shown.

You can always **reopen the popup window 🔄️** if you lost it or closed it by accident.

> [!IMPORTANT]  
> Your browser may ask you to allow popups first, or the popup may appear
> behind your main browser window.

For each page you receive **a number of options**, both as buttons and as keyboard shortcuts (the keys shown next to each option).
Which options are available depends on the configuration of your session.
Pick an option (for multiple-choice questions you may pick several) and submit to continue to the next page.

If available, additional metadata is shown to help you rate the link.

Your answers are **saved automatically** as soon as you submit them.
When you have rated every page, the application shows a "session complete" screen.

If you **stop in the middle**, you can simply reopen the link to your
session later: the next time you visit, you continue with another unrated
link.

> [!TIP]  
> Made a mistake? You can always go **back to the previous question**
> with the browser's _back_ button.

#### Got stuck?

Contact the person who sent you the link to the judging session.
